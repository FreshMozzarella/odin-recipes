Pizza Sauce

Description

Pizza sauce recipe inspired by Kne Forkish's Flour Salt Water Yeast recipe book

Key here is to use San Marzano tomatoes (and if you can find calabrian oregano, even better). Make sure to have a colander and blender (food processor work very well for this as well)

Yield: enough sauce for five 12-inch round thin pizzas

Ingredients

  • 1 (28 ounce) can whole San Marzano tomatoes
  • 1 ½ tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic (optional)
  • ½ teaspoon fine sea salt
  • ¼ teaspoon dried oregano
  • ¼ teaspoon chile flakes (optional)

Steps

  1. Place a colander over a large bowl. Drain the tomatoes for 10 to 15 minutes (Optional: can also try to use a wooden spoon to repeatedly push the tomatoes back and forth to break them into a chunky pulp to drain even more of the juice to have a more chunky texture)
  2. Put the olive oil, garlic, salt, oregano, and chile flakes in a blender. Add the tomatoes and blend until smooth and thoroughly combined
  3. Refrigerate until the day you bake your pizza
